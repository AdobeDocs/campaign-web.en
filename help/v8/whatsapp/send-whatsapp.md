# Preview and send your WhatsApp delivery {#send-whatsapp}

## Preview your WhatsApp message {#preview-whatsapp}

In the Adobe Campaign Web user interface, after you define message content, use **[!UICONTROL Simulate content]** with test profiles (or sample data) to preview personalization and shortened URLs.

Detailed steps for preview and proofs are in [Preview the message content](../preview-test/preview-content.md) and [About preview and proofs](../preview-test/preview-test.md).

## Validate your content {#whatsapp-validate}

In the Adobe Campaign Web user interface, check alerts at the top of the editor. Some are recommendations; others block send until resolved.

* **Warnings** highlight best practices (for example, empty placeholder text).

* **Errors** prevent preparation, proofs, or send until you fix them (for example, missing required template mapping).

## Send your WhatsApp delivery {#whatsapp-send}

>[!IMPORTANT]
>
>If your organization uses campaign approvals, follow your approval workflow before production send. [Learn more](../campaigns/campaign-approvals.md)

When the message is ready:

* **Standalone or campaign delivery**: In the Adobe Campaign Web user interface, open the delivery, run preparation if prompted, then use **[!UICONTROL Review & send]** and **[!UICONTROL Send]** from the delivery dashboard—same pattern as [SMS](../sms/send-sms.md).

* **Workflow**: In the Adobe Campaign Web user interface, start or resume the workflow so the delivery reaches a ready state, open the delivery from the activity, then confirm send from the delivery dashboard. [Learn more](../workflows/start-monitor-workflows.md)

In the Adobe Campaign Web user interface, track results from the delivery **[!UICONTROL Reports]** entry points and [delivery reporting](../reporting/delivery-reports.md).
